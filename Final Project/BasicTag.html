Heading Tag

Heading tags are essential elements in web development that help define the hierarchy and structure of the content on a webpage. There are six levels of headings, ranging from level 1 to level 6, where level 1 represents the most important heading (usually the main title of the page) and level 6 is the least important (used for minor sub-sections).

Types of Heading Tags:

Heading 1 (H1):

This is the main heading of a webpage, often used for the title or the most significant piece of content.

There should generally only be one H1 per page to represent the core topic.

Heading 2 (H2):

Used for subheadings or sections under the H1.

You can have multiple H2 headings on a page to break the content into main sections.

Heading 3 (H3):

A subheading that provides further breakdown within sections defined by H2 headings.

H3 helps in organizing content that is a subset of an H2 section.

Heading 4 (H4):

Less commonly used but can be applied when you need even more detailed subdivisions under an H3.

Heading 5 (H5) and Heading 6 (H6):

These are the lowest levels of headings, often used for very specific or granular sections within the content.
